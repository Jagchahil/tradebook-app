# 31: Social Operating System, how we run the organic engine

> How the daily content machine actually works, who does what, where we post, and what I need from you to run it. The goal of every post is one thing: a free-trial signup. We measure against that, not vanity likes.

---

## The division of labour, honest and clear

**What I do (daily):**
- Generate the day's posts across every angle and platform, 10 to 20 pieces, scripts, captions, hooks, hashtags, and shot lists.
- Review yesterday's numbers, find what worked and what flopped, and adapt today's batch accordingly.
- Keep a running content bank and calendar, and steer the strategy week to week.

**What you (or a tool) do:**
- The actual publishing. I cannot tap publish or hold your logins, and frankly you do not want an AI holding social passwords, that is how accounts get banned. So posting happens through either a scheduler you load, or an automation that auto-posts what I produce.
- Record any founder-on-camera or on-site clips when a script calls for it.
- Reserve the account handles (I cannot create accounts).

**The bridge for posting, pick one:**
- **Easiest:** a scheduler like Metricool or Buffer. I produce the batch, you bulk-load it once, it drips out on schedule. Cheap, simple, you stay in control.
- **Most hands-off:** connect **Zapier or Make**, and I can push posts straight into an automation that publishes them. More setup, less daily touching.
- **The analytics loop is already half-built:** **Supermetrics is connected**, which pulls performance from TikTok, Instagram, Facebook, and LinkedIn. Once the accounts exist and are linked in Supermetrics, my daily "analyse and adapt" runs on real data automatically.

## Where we post

### Phase 1, launch: one strong brand presence per core platform
Do not spread thin at the start. One good account each, posted to hard, beats ten weak ones.

- **TikTok** @lekhio, the top priority. Trades and short pain-point video do well, organic reach is still highest here for a new account.
- **Instagram** @lekhio, Reels first, plus carousels and stories.
- **Facebook** Lekhio page, plus this is where the **trade groups** live, which is where your buyers actually hang out.
- **YouTube** Shorts, the same vertical clips, plus a home for longer demos later.
- **LinkedIn** Lekhio company page, for the more organised sole trader and the "going legit" angle.

### Phase 1 also: where the trades already gather
- **Facebook groups** for UK trades and local self-employed. Value first, never spammy.
- **Reddit**, r/uktrades and similar, helpful answers that mention Lekhio only when it genuinely fits.

### Phase 2, once the machine is proven: satellite angle pages
Faceless value pages that build an audience around a pain, then funnel to Lekhio. For example a "UK Sole Trader Tax Tips" page or a "Tradesperson Money" page. These aggregate followers who are not searching for us yet. Powerful, but only add them once the main accounts are humming, because they multiply the workload.

## The angles, so no two posts feel the same

We rotate across these so the feed never looks repetitive, and so we catch different people on different days:

1. **The pain.** The shoebox of receipts, the 3am tax panic, the lost invoice.
2. **The 20-second demo.** Text a photo, it is logged. Show how stupidly easy it is.
3. **MTD explained simply.** What the government change actually means for a sole trader, no jargon.
4. **Trust and anti-scam.** Read only, we never touch your money, registered with the ICO. Trades are wary, so this matters.
5. **Time back.** "Got my evenings back," the emotional win.
6. **Quick tips.** Allowable expenses, mileage, what you can claim. Useful even if they never buy, which earns the follow.
7. **Myth busting.** "You do not need an accountant to start," "MTD is not optional."
8. **Behind the build.** Two founders building it for trades, the underdog story.
9. **Wider self-employed.** The cafe owner, the mobile hairdresser, the PT, leading with trades but spilling outward.

## The daily loop (this is what "run and adapt" means)

Every morning, automatically:
1. **Pull yesterday's numbers** (views, watch time, saves, clicks to trial, trials started). Supermetrics where connected, your quick screenshot where not.
2. **Find the signal.** Which hook, angle, and format drove the most trial signups, not just likes. Which flopped.
3. **Adapt.** Double down on what converts, cut what does not, and spin fresh variations of the winners.
4. **Generate today's batch,** 10 to 20 posts across the angles and platforms, saved as a dated file in this project folder for you or the scheduler to publish.
5. **Flag anything** that needs you, a clip to film, a comment trend worth riding, a winner worth putting ad spend behind.

Weekly, I will give you a short scoreboard: what is working, what changed, and where the trial signups are coming from.

## What we measure (tied to the projections)

Vanity is views. What pays the bills is the funnel:

- **Reach** to **profile visits** to **link clicks** to **trials started** to **paid conversions**.
- The north star is **trials started per day**, because the whole projection model runs on it.
- We watch **cost per trial** on ads and **which organic angle converts**, and move budget and effort there.
