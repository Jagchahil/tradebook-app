# 68: Go To Market and Marketing Strategy

> Written 1 July 2026. The founder has asked me (Claude) to own marketing strategy and execution, starting organic only, with weekly scheduled, posted, and reviewed content, then commissioned door to door sales, founder led business to business, and paid ads once revenue supports it. This doc is the plan, and it is the narrative behind the numbers in `Lekhio_Financial_Model.xlsx`. Global sequence: UK from mid July 2026, Europe from mid 2027, United States from 2028, rest of world and a corporate multi location product from 2029. This is a plan, not a promise. The model's blue cells are where the assumptions live and can be flexed.

---

## 1. The one idea

We sell time back to people who are on their tools all day. The message is never "accounting software". It is "text a photo of the receipt, we do the rest, and you are ready for the taxman". Every channel, every post, every doorstep pitch says the same thing in the language of the trade. The April 2026 Making Tax Digital mandate is the wind at our back: 864,000 UK sole traders and landlords are already inside it, close to 3 million follow by 2028, and construction is the single biggest self employed trade in the country at roughly 748,000 people. We are selling a legal deadline solved by a text message.

---

## 2. Positioning, in one line the trade understands

Not a tool, not a service, both. We give you the tools that do the job for you, all built in house, so you snap it, say it, or text it, and your books and tax are handled. You always approve before anything reaches HMRC. Honesty is a feature: we never say "we file your tax", we say "we prepare it, you approve it". That line survives regulators and earns trust with someone who is nervous about tax.

---

## 3. Phased channel plan

The order matters. Cheap and trust building first, paid and scalable once the funnel converts.

**Phase 0, launch, from mid July 2026.** Organic only, run by Claude. No ad spend. The job here is to prove the message converts and to build a content library and a small base of real users whose results become the next month's proof.

**Phase 1, commissioned door to door, from around September 2026.** Self employed reps who walk sites, trade counters, and estates. The commission is deliberately founder friendly and rep friendly: on a monthly plan the rep keeps the whole first month of what they sell, so they are paid immediately and we are never out of pocket, and on an annual plan the rep is paid a flat 19.99 per sale so the company keeps the rest. This makes customer acquisition cost close to one month of revenue, which is extremely efficient, and it turns selling into a self funding engine. Reps carry the same honest pitch as the content.

**Phase 1 in parallel, founder led.** Jag works business to business, stops van driving tradespeople, trade suppliers, and word of mouth. High trust, low cost, and a direct line to what the market actually asks for, which feeds back into content and product.

**Phase 2, paid ads, once revenue supports it, through 2027.** Meta, Google, and TikTok, run by Claude with full autonomy on strategy, creative, and budget within agreed guardrails. We only turn paid on when the organic and door funnels have proven the trial to paid conversion and the payback period, so we are pouring fuel on a fire that already burns, not buying growth we cannot retain.

---

## 4. The organic engine, what Claude runs each week

A fixed weekly rhythm, scheduled, posted, and reviewed, so it compounds rather than lurching.

**Platforms.** Short video first, because that is where trades scroll: TikTok, Instagram Reels, YouTube Shorts. A lighter presence on Facebook groups where local trades gather, and LinkedIn for the later corporate and accountant partnership angle.

**Content pillars, rotated.** One, the deadline: plain explainers of what MTD means for a sole trader and the dates that bite. Two, the money: what you can claim, CIS refunds most subbies are owed, the cost of getting it wrong. Three, the magic moment: short clips of a receipt photo or a voice note becoming a logged, categorised entry. Four, the trade specific angle, one video per trade, electricians, plumbers, roofers, and so on, mirroring the twenty landing pages already live on the site. Five, proof and story, once we have real, consenting users, never faked.

**Cadence.** A set number of posts per platform per week, planned on Monday, produced and scheduled across the week, and reviewed every week against a simple scoreboard: reach, saves, profile visits, link clicks, trials started. What works gets more of the budget of attention, what does not gets cut. I run this and report the scoreboard to you weekly.

**Guardrails, non negotiable.** Every claim is honest and compliant. Never imply HMRC endorsement, never say we file your tax, no fake testimonials, no scare content that crosses into misinformation. Anything published on the company's behalf follows the same approval discipline as the rest of the product. Full autonomy on strategy and creative, inside those lines.

---

## 5. Global expansion sequence

UK first, and win it properly, because a tax product is country specific and depth beats breadth. Europe from mid 2027, taken country by country as each one's digital tax and rules are built into the engine, not all at once. The United States from 2028, a large and harder market with state level complexity, entered once the UK and first EU markets are paying for themselves. Rest of world and a corporate, multi location product from 2029, for larger companies operating across borders who want the simple way to do tax at scale. That corporate market is harder to crack and higher value, and it is a deliberate later act, not a distraction from the trades wedge that gets us there.

---

## 6. How this maps to the model

The spreadsheet is driver based, and every driver here is a blue, editable cell in `Lekhio_Financial_Model.xlsx`.

- The 2026 launch tab builds month by month, August to December, from three channels: organic, door to door, and founder or referral. It starts deliberately small, a few hundred paying customers by year end, because organic compounds slowly and reps only start in September.
- Years 1 to 5 scale new customer additions aggressively as paid ads switch on and each new region opens, blended into one number per year so the model stays readable. ARPU rises gently as annual plans and later corporate customers mix in.
- Costs are modelled simply and transparently: AI and infrastructure per active customer, payment fees as a share of revenue, commissions on new sales, marketing and ad spend, and team and operating costs. Change any of them and the whole model recalculates.

On the current aggressive base case, the shape is: roughly 700 paying customers and about 20,000 pounds of revenue in the 2026 launch stub, about 4,900 customers and 1 million pounds of annual recurring revenue by the end of Year 1, and scaling to the order of 150,000 customers and mid tens of millions of annual recurring revenue by Year 5. Treat the later years as directional. The near term months and Year 1 are where the assumptions are most grounded, and they are the ones to hold me to.

---

## 7. The metrics I will watch, and report to you

A short, honest scoreboard beats a vanity dashboard. Weekly and monthly I track: trials started by channel, trial to paid conversion, customer acquisition cost by channel, payback period in months, monthly churn, monthly recurring revenue and annual recurring revenue, and the channel mix of new customers. The single most important early number is trial to paid conversion, because it tells us whether the message works before we spend a penny on ads. The second is payback period on the door to door channel, because the commission design should make it about one month, and if it does, we scale reps hard.

And a decision, when the time comes, on the paid ads budget ceiling, after which I run within it. Everything else, strategy, calendar, creative direction, and the weekly scoreboard, I own.
