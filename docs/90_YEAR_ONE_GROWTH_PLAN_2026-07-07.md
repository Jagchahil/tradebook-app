# 90: Year one growth plan, the road to Jersey (7 July 2026)

> The plan to grow Lekhio from launch to roughly 6,500 paying subscribers inside 12 months, the level that lets Jag pay himself £250k and qualify for Jersey's Skilled High Earner route (see doc 89). Real numbers, real channels, and the honest funding truth. This is a plan, not a promise. Growth depends on execution, product retention, and fuel. Writing rule holds: no em dashes, no en dashes, no hyphens used as dashes.

## The target, and why it is 6,500 subscribers

Jersey's Skilled High Earner route needs you to show £250,000 of taxable income you can pay yourself, plus a track record and forward projections. At a blended £12 a month per subscriber (£144 a year), the business needs enough recurring revenue that a £250k draw is comfortable while still funding growth. That lands at roughly:

- About 6,500 paying subscribers, which is about £78k monthly recurring revenue and roughly £935k of annual recurring revenue.
- At that size the company can pay you £250k and still reinvest, which is exactly what the application wants to see.

The subscriber count is the one number that matters. Everything below is about getting there.

## The core assumptions (challenge these, they drive everything)

| Lever | Assumption | Note |
|---|---|---|
| Price | £12.99 a month, £129 a year | Blended to about £12 a month per user |
| Trial to paid | 20% | 14 day trial, no card. Push annual plans to lift cash |
| Monthly churn | 5% | Typical small business SaaS. Retention is the whole game |
| Paid CAC (per paying customer) | £70 falling to £50 | UK SaaS paid social is £25 to £45 per lead, then trial conversion |
| Organic and referral CAC | £0 to £25 | Slow to build, cheap, and the healthiest growth |
| Commission B2B CAC | £25 to £40 per signup | Paid to reps only on a real conversion |

Two honest truths up front. Retention matters more than acquisition: at 5% monthly churn you replace a third of your base every year just to stand still, so the product keeping people is what makes the maths work. And you cannot out-spend a leaky bucket, so the first job is that people who join, stay.

## Channel strategy, sequenced in three phases

### Phase 1, months 1 to 3: organic ignition (low cash)

The goal is proof, testimonials, and the cheapest subscribers you will ever get.

- Free tax tools and the trade pages already built (39 professions, /for/[trade]) tuned for search terms real tradespeople type: "CIS tax calculator", "self assessment for a sole trader", "how much tax will I pay self employed". This is compounding, free traffic.
- The WhatsApp referral loop turned on from day one. A happy plumber who texts a receipt and gets his tax answer is your best salesman. Reward both sides only on a real signup.
- Short vertical video, founder led, on TikTok, Instagram Reels, YouTube Shorts. Not polished ads, real "here is a receipt, watch it become a tax deduction in ten seconds" clips. Trades content performs when it is useful and blunt.
- Trade Facebook groups, Reddit, and forums. Show up as a helper, not a spammer.
- Founder led B2B on foot: walk into plumbing and electrical merchants (the trade counters), talk to trade associations, CIS heavy subcontractor gangs. Every conversation is a testimonial or a lesson.

Exit month 3 target: about 900 paying subscribers. Cash spend low, mostly your time.

### Phase 2, months 4 to 6: turn on paid and recruit reps

Now you have testimonials and know which messages land. Pour fuel on the fire.

- Meta ads (Facebook and Instagram) targeting trades interests and lookalikes of your early subscribers. Trades are very reachable here. Budget scales from about £10k to £45k a month across the phase.
- Google Search for high intent terms. Someone typing "CIS tax app" or "bookkeeping app for tradesmen" is ready to buy. Smaller volume, higher intent, strong return. Budget about £5k to £15k a month.
- TikTok Spark ads built from your best performing organic clips. Cheap reach, lower intent, good for the top of the funnel.
- Recruit 3 to 5 commission only reps who already touch trades: people selling into merchants, trade associations, or accountants who serve subcontractors. Pay only on a real signup. This is your leverage channel.

Exit month 6 target: about 3,850 paying subscribers, roughly £46k monthly recurring revenue.

### Phase 3, months 7 to 12: scale what works, add brand

Double down on the two or three channels with the best return, and let revenue fund the spend.

- Scale paid to what the numbers justify, roughly £50k to £60k a month, only while CAC stays below your payback line.
- Grow the rep team to 10 to 15 hungry closers, with a simple leaderboard and clear commission.
- Billboard and ad van brand test in 2 or 3 trade heavy areas, placed near merchant districts and job sites. Be honest that billboards are weak for direct signups (hard to attribute, and a UK 48 sheet is about £425 a week regional, £750 to £1,250 in London). Treat them as brand and trust lift that makes the paid ads convert better, not as a primary acquisition channel. Cap the test at £30k to £60k and kill it if it does not lift the other channels.
- Referral compounding: by now the installed base is your biggest engine. Keep the loop sharp.

Exit month 12 target: about 8,000 to 9,000 paying subscribers, comfortably past the 6,500 you need.

Subscriber count is net of 5% monthly churn. Milestone crossing 6,500 happens around month 8.

| 3 | 500 | ~900 | ~£11k | Organic |
| 6 | 1,300 | ~3,850 | ~£46k | Organic plus paid plus reps |

At month 12 that is roughly £1.2m to £1.3m of annual recurring revenue, and the ability to pay yourself £250k while still growing. That is the Jersey gate, cleared.

## The honest crux: funding the ramp

This is the part most plans hide, so here it is straight.

The plan reaches the milestone in 8 to 12 months only if the paid ramp is fuelled. The problem is timing. In months 1 to 5 your revenue is small (from a few thousand a month climbing toward £40k a month), but the paid plan wants £30k to £47k a month by months 4 to 6. Early revenue cannot pay for that spend. So you have two real choices:

- Path A, pure bootstrap. Only spend what revenue throws off. Safer, no outside money, but the paid ramp follows revenue instead of leading it, so the milestone likely lands at 14 to 18 months, and the Jersey move slips to 2028.
- Path B, fund the gap. Put in roughly £150k to £250k up front, from your own capital, a revenue based finance facility, or a small pre-seed or angel round, to front load paid in months 3 to 7. Revenue then takes over and repays the momentum. This is what makes the 12 month target real.

A lever that helps either path: push the £129 annual plan hard. Every annual signup pulls a year of cash forward, which is exactly the fuel a young company needs, and annual subscribers churn less too.

## Reinvest now, draw £250k later (resolving the contradiction)

You said reinvest all profits heavily, and you also need to show £250k of income to qualify for Jersey. Those only seem to conflict. The resolution:

- During the UK build year you reinvest almost everything. You do not pay yourself £250k in the UK year, and you should not, the money is better spent growing.
- You qualify for Jersey on the run rate plus forward projections, which prove the business can pay you £250k. Once you move, you flip the dial from full reinvest to drawing your £250k salary, and by then the business supports both a sensible growth budget and your pay.

So the plan is: reinvest to reach scale, then move and draw. The build year funds the move.

## What I would prioritise, ruthlessly

If you did only four things, do these:

1. Retention first. Get monthly churn from 5% toward 3%. It is cheaper than any ad and it compounds. Onboarding, the WhatsApp habit, and the "your tax bill just went down" moment are what keep people.
2. The referral loop. Your product lives in WhatsApp, the most shareable place on earth. A great referral mechanic can carry a third of your growth at almost no cash cost.
3. One paid channel mastered before the second. Get Meta to a stable CAC below your payback line, then add Google, then TikTok. Do not spread thin.
4. Commission reps into merchants and associations. This is the unfair advantage a generic SaaS cannot copy, because trades buy on trust and word of mouth.

Billboards last, and only as brand support once the machine works.

## The numbers you watch every week

- New trials, trial to paid conversion, and paying subscribers added.
- Blended CAC and CAC by channel, against a payback target of under 6 months.
- Monthly churn and net revenue retention.
- MRR and the run rate toward £78k a month.
- Cash in the bank and months of runway at current burn.

## Risks and honest caveats

- A near identical rival (Accounted, "Penny") is already live and files MTD, which we do not yet. Our edge is depth (trades and CIS), the Rakha assistant, voice first, and the approval gate honesty. Growth has to lean on that difference, not just price.
- If retention is worse than 5% monthly, the whole ramp needs more adds to stand still, and the milestone slips. Fix retention before scaling spend.
- CAC could come in higher than £50 to £70. If it does, the funding need rises. Watch it weekly and do not scale a channel that is not paying back.
- The 12 month target assumes the funding gap is bridged. Bootstrap is fine, it is just slower, and the Jersey date moves with it.
- These figures are planning estimates to steer by and to brief any investor or adviser. They are not guarantees.
