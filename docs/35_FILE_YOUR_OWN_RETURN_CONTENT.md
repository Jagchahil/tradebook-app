## Part 1. The universal step by step (the walkthrough content)

### Step 0. Know if you even file the old way, or the new MTD way
- If your **self-employed turnover is under £50,000**, you file the normal Self Assessment return once a year, as below. This is most sole traders today.
- If your **turnover is £50,000 or more**, from **6 April 2026 you must use Making Tax Digital for Income Tax**, which means keeping digital records and sending **four quarterly updates** plus a final declaration, instead of one return. The first quarter, 6 April to 5 July 2026, is due by **7 August 2026**. The threshold drops to £30,000 in April 2027 and £20,000 in April 2028, so this is coming for nearly everyone.
- Either way, Lekhio keeps the digital records that both routes now expect. The page should detect which camp the reader is in with one question, "do you turn over more than £50k?"

### Step 1. Register as self-employed and get your UTR
- First time only. Register with HMRC as a sole trader for Self Assessment. You will get a **Unique Taxpayer Reference (UTR)** in the post, allow **2 to 3 weeks**.
- Deadline to register: by **5 October** after the end of your first tax year. For 2025/26 that is **5 October 2026**.
- You also set up a **Government Gateway** user ID and password. Keep them safe, you need them every year.

### Step 2. Gather what you need
- Your **UTR** and Government Gateway login.
- Your **National Insurance number**.
- Your **total business income (turnover)** for the tax year, 6 April to 5 April.
- Your **total allowable expenses** (see Part 2 and your trade list in Part 3).
- Records of any **other income**, employment (P60 or P45), interest, dividends, and so on.
- If you keep records with Lekhio, these numbers are already totalled and waiting. This is the whole point.

### Step 3. Log in and start the return
- Go to the HMRC Self Assessment service and sign in with your Government Gateway.
- Select the tax year you are filing, for example **2025 to 2026**.
- The return is the **SA100** main form, plus the **self-employment pages, SA103**. There is a short version (SA103S) if your turnover is under the VAT threshold, and a full version (SA103F) above it. The online system picks the right one as you answer questions.

### Step 4. Fill in the self-employment pages
- Enter your **business details**, name and what you do.
- Enter your **turnover**, the total you invoiced or were paid, before expenses.
- Enter your **allowable expenses**, either as one total or broken into categories. Categories are easier if HMRC ever asks questions.
- Note the **£1,000 trading allowance**: if your total income from self-employment is under £1,000 you may not need to report it, and if your expenses are tiny you can claim the flat £1,000 instead of actual expenses. Most working trades are well past this and claim real expenses.

### Step 5. Add any other income, then let HMRC do the maths
- Add employment income, savings interest, and anything else that applies.
- The online return **calculates your tax and National Insurance for you**. You do not work it out by hand. It applies your personal allowance, the Class 4 National Insurance on your profits, and shows the final figure.

### Step 6. Check, submit, and save the proof
- Review the calculation. Check the numbers match your records.
- **Submit**. HMRC confirms receipt on screen straight away. Save or screenshot that confirmation.

### Step 7. Pay what you owe, by 31 January
- The deadline to **file online and pay** for 2025/26 is **31 January 2027**.
- If your bill is over £1,000 you will usually also make **payments on account**, advance payments towards next year, half by 31 January and half by 31 July. The return tells you the amounts. Budget for this, it surprises first timers.
- Set the money aside through the year. Lekhio's set-aside estimate is built for exactly this.

### The deadlines, in one place
- Register by **5 October** after your first tax year.
- Paper return by **31 October**.
- **Online return and payment by 31 January**.
- Miss it and it is an automatic **£100 penalty**, then daily penalties and interest after that. Filing on time is free and easy, so we send the reminder.

## Part 2. Universal allowable expenses (every trade claims these)

The golden rule, repeated on the page: a cost must be **wholly and exclusively** for the business. Genuine costs cut your tax. Personal spending does not count.

- **Materials and stock** used on jobs.
- **Tools and equipment**, often the full cost in the year of purchase.
- **Vehicle costs**, either a share of running costs or the simple **mileage rate, 55p a mile for the first 10,000 business miles from April 2026**, then 25p.
- **Protective clothing and safety gear**, boots, gloves, hi-vis, hard hats.
- **Phone and broadband**, the business share.
- **Insurance**, public liability, tools, professional indemnity.
- **Trade body and certification fees**.
- **Use of home** for quotes, admin, and paperwork.
- **Accountant or bookkeeping software** fees, which includes Lekhio.
- **Bank charges** on the business account.
- **Advertising and a website**.
- **Training** that maintains your trade skills.
- **Subcontractor payments**, where relevant (and CIS for construction, see below).

## Part 3. The per-trade expenses layer (what swaps in when you pick your trade)

On top of the universal list, here is what each trade typically claims. This is the content behind "select your trade."

- **Electricians.** Cable, fittings, consumer units, test equipment (and its calibration), 18th Edition and certification scheme fees (NICEIC, NAPIT), PAT testing kit, van racking.
- **Plumbers.** Pipe, fittings, copper and plastic stock, leak detection and pressure testing kit, blow torch and consumables, WaterSafe or scheme membership.
- **Builders.** Aggregates, cement, timber, fixings, plant hire, skip hire and waste disposal, scaffold hire, site PPE, CITB and CIS handling.
- **Plasterers.** Plaster, beading, boards, mixing equipment, stilts, dust sheets, tower or scaffold hire.
- **Roofers.** Tiles, felt, battens, lead, harnesses and fall-arrest gear, scaffold and tower hire, ladder and roof ladder costs.
- **Joiners and carpenters.** Timber and sheet materials, ironmongery, power tools and blades, dust extraction, workshop costs if you have one.
- **Decorators.** Paint, fillers, brushes and rollers, sundries, dust sheets, sanding and spray equipment, access towers.
- **Tilers.** Tiles, adhesive and grout, trims, cutters and mixing kit, knee pads, levelling systems.
- **Gas engineers.** Parts and fittings, flue gas analyser and its calibration, Gas Safe registration, tools, manuals and standards.
- **Scaffolders.** Tube, fittings and boards, harnesses and PPE, transport of materials, CISRS card and training, vehicle and trailer costs.
- **Groundworkers.** Aggregates and concrete, drainage materials, plant and digger hire, fuel for plant, setting out kit, site welfare.
- **Landscapers.** Plants and turf, aggregates and paving, mowers and machinery, green waste and tip fees, fuel for equipment, machine servicing.

A note for construction trades: if you work under the **Construction Industry Scheme (CIS)**, contractors deduct tax from your pay at source, and that deducted tax comes off your final bill or is refunded through the return. The walkthrough flags this for the relevant trades.
