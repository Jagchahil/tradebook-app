## The name

**Lekhio**. From *lekh*, the Punjabi and Sanskrit root meaning writing, accounts, and records. So the name quietly means "your books" in the founders' own heritage, while the modern ending makes it read as a clean, premium app. Capital L, the rest lowercase: **Lekhio**.

The company is **LEKHIO LTD**, company number **17329341**, incorporated 8 July 2026. Jag holds the shares personally.

> **Corrected 11 July 2026.** This line used to say Lekhio was "owned by the parent company Satluj Ventures Ltd". **There is no parent company and there must not be one.** Doc 92 is explicit: one shareholder, one class of ordinary shares, **no holding company**, so that a later move to Jersey stays clean (doc 89). That is the structure that was actually incorporated. A stale line like this ends up copied onto an App Store listing or a legal form, so it is fixed here rather than left to be believed.

**Satluj Ventures is dropped. Lekhio is the sole name.** A "Lekhio Group" may exist later, if and when the group in doc 98 is real, but there is no holding company today and no second brand. The name still comes from the river Satluj, which runs through the founders' villages, and the river is where the logo and the colours come from. That is heritage, not a corporate structure.

Written as "Lekhio" in all copy, UI, and code.

## Who it is for

Lekhio is for anyone who works for themselves in the UK. Tradespeople first in our advertising, electricians, plumbers, builders, but the product is built for every sole trader: cafes, barbers and hairdressers, drivers, cleaners, market traders, tutors, freelancers, carers, photographers, anyone who sends an invoice or keeps a receipt. The marketing can speak to trades. The product never shuts anyone out.

## Logo

A clean **Lekhio** wordmark with a single flowing river line beneath it. The river runs left to right and shifts from river blue into saffron gold, the water meeting the fields of Punjab. It is the visual tie to Satluj. Use the wordmark with the river on light backgrounds. On dark, the wordmark is white and the river keeps its gradient. The standalone river mark can be used as an app icon or favicon.

Keep it minimal. Never crowd it. Never add effects beyond the gradient.

## Colour palette

Drawn from Punjab: the deep blue of the rivers, the saffron gold of the fields, on warm paper.

| Name | Hex | Usage |
|---|---|---|
| Ink | `#111111` | Primary text, dark sections |
| River | `#1B59A6` | Primary brand, CTAs, links, active states |
| River deep | `#134277` | CTA hover, dark accents |
| River tint | `#E9F1FA` | Soft fills, highlights, hover backgrounds |
| Saffron | `#E0A33E` | Accent only. The river-to-gold, key highlights, small flourishes |
| Saffron deep | `#C9842A` | Accent hover, gold text on light |
| Paper | `#FBFAF7` | Page background, warm not stark white |
| Surface | `#F2F0EA` | Cards, inputs |
| Line | `#E7E3D9` | Borders, dividers |
| White | `#FFFFFF` | On river, on dark |

Functional: Success `#16A34A`, Danger `#DC2626`, Muted text `#6B7280`.

**Rule:** River is the brand. Saffron is a guest, used sparingly for warmth and the river motif. Never let saffron become the dominant colour.

**Primary:** background River `#1B59A6`, text white, radius 12px, hover River deep `#134277`.
**Secondary:** transparent, 1px border Ink, text Ink, hover Surface fill.
**On dark:** white button with Ink text, or River with white text.

## Motion

Calm and premium, never busy. Gentle fade-and-rise as sections enter. The hero river line flows slowly. Hovers lift cards a touch and deepen the colour. Nothing bounces, nothing flashes. Motion should feel like water, not like a toy.

---

## Voice and tone

**Lekhio is:** Direct. Confident. Calm. On your side.
**Lekhio is not:** Corporate. Jargon-heavy. Condescending. Salesy.

Write like you are talking to a busy self-employed person who has thirty seconds. Short sentences. No jargon. Never "utilise", "leverage", "seamless", or "solution". Always say what the product *does*, not what it *is*.

**Good:** "Snap a receipt. Lekhio logs it."
**Bad:** "Our AI-powered solution streamlines your expense management workflow."

---

## Don'ts

- Never all caps (LEKHIO) or all lowercase (lekhio) for the wordmark.
- Never make saffron the primary colour.
- Never put the wordmark on a busy background without a clear contrast layer.
- Never imply HMRC endorsement.

---

## Punctuation rules

- No em dashes, no en dashes, no hyphens used as dashes.
- Use a full stop or rewrite the sentence instead.
- Applies to all copy: website, app, WhatsApp messages, emails, docs.
