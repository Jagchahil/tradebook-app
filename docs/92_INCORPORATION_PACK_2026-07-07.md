# 92: Lekhio Ltd incorporation pack (7 July 2026)

> A fill in the blanks pack so Jag can incorporate Lekhio Ltd directly at Companies House in one sitting, structured to stay clean for a Jersey move in about three or more years (doc 89). This is information to work from, not legal, tax, or immigration advice. Have your accountant sign off the SIC codes and share structure before filing. Writing rule holds: no em dashes, no en dashes, no hyphens used as dashes.

## Why a UK Ltd now, and why kept simple

A UK private company limited by shares is the right vehicle today. A Jersey company run from your desk in London would still be UK tax resident through central management and control, so registering in Jersey now buys nothing and adds cost (doc 89). You incorporate a genuine UK company now, hold the shares yourself, and keep the structure boring so that a later residence move, or a redomicile of the company to Jersey, stays clean. The three things that keep it clean: one shareholder (you), one class of shares (ordinary), and no holding company. Do not add alphabet shares, multiple classes, or a parent entity now.

## Where to file and what it costs

Two routes, both fine for this structure:

- **Direct at Companies House**, "Register a company". Fee is 100 pounds (up from 50 on 1 February 2026), paid by card, usually incorporated the same working day.
- **Through Tide (recommended for you).** Tide is an Authorised Corporate Service Provider, so it forms the company and opens the free business account in one flow, verifies your identity on Companies House's behalf, and charges 14.99 and covers the rest of the fee. Certificate typically within one business day. Tide only accepts a single shareholder, which is exactly your structure, so that restriction is a non issue here. See the "Doing it through Tide" section below.

Either way, you do the filing, the identity verification, and the payment yourself.

## The fields, filled in

**1. Company name.**
- Proposed: `Lekhio Ltd` (or `Lekhio Limited`, identical in law, "Ltd" is just the short form).
- Before filing, run three checks:
  - Companies House name availability checker, to confirm it is free and not "same as" an existing name.
  - The name does not contain a sensitive or restricted word needing permission.
  - IPO trade mark register (separate from Companies House), so the word "Lekhio" is not already a registered mark in your classes. Registering the company name does not give you the trade mark, so consider filing a UK trade mark for "Lekhio" in the relevant classes (software, class 9, and financial or bookkeeping services, class 36 or 35) once incorporated.

**2. Company type.** Private company limited by shares.

**3. Registered office address.** Must be a UK address (England and Wales). It is on the public register.
- DECISION FOR YOU: your home address (free, but public and awkward given you plan to leave the UK), or a registered office service address (about 30 to 50 pounds a year, keeps your home off the public record). Given the relocation plan and basic privacy, a service address is the cleaner choice. Many formation agents and accountants include one.
- The rules from 2024 require a registered office to be an "appropriate address" where post is acknowledged, so a plain PO box will not do, but a service address provider is fine.

**4. Director.**
- You, sole director.
- Provide: full name, date of birth, nationality, occupation, and a service address (use the registered office or a service address, not your home, for the public record). Your usual residential address is filed too but is kept private by Companies House.

**5. Person with significant control (PSC).**
- You.
- Nature of control to tick: "holds more than 75% of the shares" and "holds more than 75% of the voting rights" (as sole shareholder you also hold the right to appoint or remove the majority of directors).

**6. Shareholder and share structure (keep it deliberately simple).**
- One shareholder: you, personally (not through any other company).
- One class: ordinary shares, full voting and dividend rights, no special conditions.
- Suggested allotment: 100 ordinary shares of 1 pound each, all issued to you, shown as fully paid (or leave unpaid and pay them up, your accountant can advise which they prefer for the first accounts). One hundred shares gives clean round percentages if you ever bring in an investor, without needing to subdivide.
- Statement of capital: 100 shares, aggregate nominal value 100 pounds, one currency (GBP), one class.
- Prescribed particulars for the ordinary class: one vote per share, full dividend rights, full rights to capital on winding up, not redeemable.

**7. Articles of association.** Use the standard model articles. They are the simplest, best understood, and exactly what keeps the structure clean for a later move. Do not adopt bespoke articles now.

**8. SIC codes (nature of business).** Advisory, confirm with your accountant. Lekhio is a software product that performs bookkeeping, so lead with software and add the domain:
- `62012` Business and domestic software development (primary)
- `62020` Information technology consultancy activities
- `69202` Bookkeeping activities
- `63990` Other information service activities not elsewhere classified (optional)

**9. Identity verification.** From 2025, directors and PSCs must verify their identity with Companies House (via GOV.UK One Login or an authorised agent). Do this as part of, or right after, incorporation. Have photo ID ready.

## Doing it through Tide (the recommended route)

Tide bundles the incorporation and the free business account, which collapses two of your gates into one sitting. Why it fits:

- Single shareholder only, which is exactly your clean structure (you, personally). No compromise.
- It is an Authorised Corporate Service Provider, so it does your Companies House identity verification for you (have photo ID ready).

- 14.99 all in, versus 100 direct.
- Optional virtual registered office in Central London for a small monthly fee, which settles the registered office decision below and keeps your home address off the public register.

Watch items while going through the Tide flow, so the structure stays clean:

- **Shares.** When it asks about share capital, set one class of ordinary shares. If it lets you choose the number, use 100 ordinary shares of 1 pound. If it defaults to a single share, that is still a valid clean structure (one ordinary share, you as sole holder), just less tidy for a future investor split. Either is fine, 100 is preferred.
- **Shareholder.** Confirm you are the sole shareholder, held personally, not through any other entity.
- **SIC codes.** Enter the four from field 8 above (62012 primary).
- **Registered office.** Choose Tide's virtual London address (for privacy given the relocation plan) or your own. Not your home if you can avoid it.
- **PSC.** You, more than 75% control.
- **Name.** Do the three name checks in field 1 first (Companies House availability, sensitive words, IPO trade mark) before you commit in the Tide flow.

Everything else in the fields section still applies. The Tide flow just presents the same Companies House fields inside its wizard.

## Right after the certificate lands

- **Business bank account.** Open a company account (Tide, Mettle, or Starling, all free) using the company registration number (CRN). Keep company money strictly separate from personal money. This matters for the Jersey plan: cars and houses are bought personally from taxed dividends, never through the company (doc 89), and clean separation is what makes that defensible.
- **Register for Corporation Tax.** Within 3 months of starting to trade, register with HMRC. Your accountant can do this with the CRN.
- **PAYE.** If you take any salary, register as an employer for PAYE. A small salary plus dividends is the sensible extraction mix while UK based (doc 89).
- **VAT.** Not required yet, but watch the 90k rolling threshold as revenue grows.
- **Confirmation statement and accounts.** Note the annual confirmation statement (34 pounds a year online) and the first accounts due date. Your accountant will diary these.

## The Jersey clean-records brief for your accountant

Give your accountant this short brief on day one so records are clean from the start for a later residence move:

- The intention is a personal relocation to Jersey in roughly three or more years, once Lekhio can pay you about 250k a year (the Skilled High Earner route, doc 89). This is a move of where you live, not a paper structure.
- Keep evidence that the company's central management and control is in the UK now: board decisions minuted, key decisions taken and recorded in the UK. This makes the company clearly UK tax resident today, which is correct, and gives a clean baseline for any future assessment of whether and when it should redomicile.
- Keep the cap table simple and documented: one shareholder, one ordinary class, no holding company. Flag that a redomicile of Lekhio Ltd to Jersey may be assessed later by a UK and Jersey cross border adviser, so do not create structures now that would complicate it.
- Model extraction as a small salary plus dividends while UK resident. Do not buy vehicles or property through the company (a company car benefit charge of about 37% of list price applies every year); buy personally from taxed dividends.
- When income approaches 250k, the plan is to engage a Jersey immigration lawyer and a UK and Jersey cross border tax adviser to map the exact departure dates and the Statutory Residence Test split year. Note this in the file now so nothing done in the meantime cuts across it.
