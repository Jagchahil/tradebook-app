# 89: Jersey relocation memo (7 July 2026)

> A working plan for Jag to legally keep more of what Lekhio makes by relocating to Jersey, on his own route, with the option to return to the UK later. This is information, not financial, tax, or immigration advice. Nothing here should be acted on without sign off from a UK and Jersey cross border tax adviser, a Jersey immigration lawyer, and the relevant medical regulator. Writing rule holds: no em dashes, no en dashes, no hyphens used as dashes.

## Why Jersey, and the one principle that makes it work

The only real lever that lowers your tax is your own tax residency, not where the company is registered. While you live in the UK, the UK taxes your worldwide income no matter where Lekhio sits. So the plan is not a paper structure. It is a genuine move of where you live.

Jersey is the best fit for you because:

- It is the closest low tax jurisdiction to your London family. City Airport or Gatwick to Jersey is about 50 minutes, so you would be closer than someone living in Manchester.
- Personal income tax is capped at a flat 20%, with no capital gains tax, no inheritance tax, and no stamp duty on share sales.
- Most Jersey trading companies pay 0% corporation tax under the "zero ten" system. Only financial services firms pay 10%. So there is a real question, for an adviser, about whether Lekhio itself should be based in Jersey too.
- A new residency route, live since April 2026, fits a founder relocating in his own right without a spouse or a local employer.

The honest trade offs: Jersey housing is expensive and access is gated by residential status, the cost of living is high, and the Sikh community is small. You have said the small community is acceptable as long as you can return to the UK, which you can, on the timing set out below.

## The goal, stated plainly

Keep as much of what Lekhio makes as legally possible. Fund a good life now (a car and a house), and build the business into something large. The genuinely life changing number, the £500m type outcome, is the equity value of the company, most of which stays on paper until you sell or draw it down over years. Jersey's zero capital gains tax is what protects that number if a sale ever happens.

## Your own way in: the Skilled High Earner route

This is the door that fits you, with no reliance on a partner and no need for a Jersey employer.

Requirements:

- Establish your business in Jersey (Lekhio) and work it full time from the island, defined as more than 25 hours a week, actively generating earned income.
- Maintain Jersey taxable income of at least £250,000 a year, for up to ten years or until you reach permanent "Entitled" status.
- Buy or lease a property valued at £2m or more. Leasing keeps this lighter than buying outright.
- You must not already be resident in Jersey or own property there at the time you apply.
- Licensed permission is granted for five years, then reviewed and renewed for a further five years if you still meet the terms.

The key insight: the £250,000 income threshold is essentially the same milestone as being able to fund the car. On the earlier projections, paying yourself £250k or more from Lekhio lands at roughly 5,000 paying subscribers. So the same moment the business can fund your life is roughly the moment it qualifies you for your own Jersey residency. One milestone, not two.

## The residency ladder for you

1. Now to about 5,000 subscribers. You stay UK based, keep building, and pay UK tax. You cannot jump to Jersey before you are earning the £250k, so this stage is unavoidable. Take a sensible small salary plus dividends in the meantime.
2. At £250k or more of income. The Skilled High Earner route opens. You move on your own business, pay Jersey's flat 20% ceiling, no capital gains tax, no inheritance tax. Lease a qualifying property rather than buying £2m outright to keep it lighter.
3. If Lekhio gets genuinely large. Personal wealth over £10m and income over £1.25m unlocks High Value Residency (the "2(1)(e)" regime), where the first £1.25m of income is taxed at 20% and everything above it at just 1%. This is the endgame cap, requiring a qualifying property purchase of roughly £3.5m for a house or £1.75m for an apartment, and a minimum annual Jersey tax contribution of £250,000.

## The tax picture, side by side

- UK now: income tax up to 45%, plus dividend tax up to 39.35% on money taken out of the company, plus 25% corporation tax on company profit, plus capital gains tax of roughly 24% on a sale (with some relief on the first £1m).
- Jersey, standard resident (Skilled High Earner): income tax capped at 20%, 0% capital gains tax, 0% inheritance tax, 0% stamp duty on share sales, and most likely 0% corporation tax for the company under zero ten.

That is the difference you have been feeling. It is real, and it is legal, precisely because it depends on you actually living there.

## Coming back to the UK: the five year rule

You can come back. The timing is the one thing you cannot bend.

- The temporary non residence rules catch you if your time away is five years or less. To be safe, be genuinely non resident for more than five complete tax years, then return. In practice: do a clean five full tax years in Jersey and come home in the sixth. Cut it shorter and the UK claws back tax on all the dividends and gains you took while you were away, as if you had never left.
- Give it the extra margin and you are clear. The money you build up and extract during the Jersey years stays untaxed by the UK.

Two things that make the return clean rather than just on paper:

- You must have actually been non resident that whole time. Real move, UK days kept under the Statutory Residence Test limits every year, ties managed. Because you will still have close family here, and possibly property, your UK ties are high, which means your allowed UK days are low, possibly as few as 45 to 90 a year. Jersey's closeness makes it easy to overstay, so this has to be tracked carefully.
- "Coming back" means back into the UK tax net going forward. The five year rule protects what you earned and drew during the Jersey years. Any new income after you return is UK taxed as normal.

The shape that works: five plus clean years out, return in year six, keep everything from the away years.

## Your partner's medical career, kept independent

You do not need to rely on each other's status, and you are not married yet, so each of you moves in your own right. For a doctor, Jersey does not run its own medical exams. To practise there she must be registered with the GMC, which she already would be, and then join the Jersey medical practitioners register. So it is a registration step on her existing GMC registration, not re sitting exams. She would confirm her specialty's exact route with the Jersey Health department. She can follow whenever you both choose, on her own footing.

## What does not work, so we do not waste time on it

- Paper residence. Keeping your home, life, and most of your days in the UK while claiming to live in Jersey. The Statutory Residence Test catches this and the tax is back plus penalties.
- Moving only the company while you stay UK resident. The UK still taxes your worldwide income.
- Buying a Ferrari through the company. A company car benefit charge of about 37% of list price applies every year. Buy it personally from taxed dividends instead.
- A car rental business set up mainly so you can drive the cars. HMRC treats availability for private use as a benefit, and a business built to disguise a personal supercar as a red flag. Do not do it.

## The plan, in phases

- Phase 1, now: build Lekhio toward £250k of annual income for you (roughly 5,000 paying subscribers). Stay UK based and compliant. Keep clean records, which your own product helps with.
- Phase 2, as you approach £250k: engage a Jersey immigration lawyer and a UK and Jersey cross border tax adviser. Map the exact departure dates, the Statutory Residence Test split year, and whether Lekhio itself should redomicile to Jersey.
- Phase 3, move: apply on the Skilled High Earner route, lease a qualifying property, establish the business in Jersey, work it full time from the island.
- Phase 4, the away years: keep UK days under the limit every year, extract and bank the bulk of the money during this window, stay genuinely non resident for more than five complete tax years.
- Phase 5, return if you want to: come home in year six or later with the away years' money intact, and accept normal UK tax on income going forward. Or graduate to High Value Residency if the business has become large enough to justify staying.
