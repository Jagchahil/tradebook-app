## Why a free trial changes everything, up and down

**Up:** with no money asked for up front, signups multiply, especially when you are posting 10 to 20 times a day and casting wider than trades. The trial lets the product sell itself, and you told me the product quality is there, which is exactly what makes a trial convert.

**Down, and you must respect this:**
- **Revenue lags 30 days.** A July signup pays nothing until late August. The trial pushes real cash to the right.
- **Only a fraction convert.** The whole model now swings on **trial-to-paid conversion**. I have modelled 25% conservative, 30% base, 33% aggressive, 38% blitz. If real conversion is 15%, the paying base roughly halves. This is the single most important number in the entire forecast, more than reach, more than posting volume.
- **Free trials cost you money.** Every triallist uses Claude and WhatsApp with no revenue yet. Modelled as a servicing cost. Thousands of free trials is thousands of pounds of AI and messaging before they pay. Manageable, but real.

So the trial is the right call, and it is why aggressive numbers are credible at all. Just know the lever moved from "how many sign up" to "how many of those actually pay on day 31."

---

## What has to be true for Aggressive and Blitz to land

These columns are achievable, not fantasy, but only if all of the following hold. I am your accountant here, so I will be straight: each one is a genuine if.

1. **The 10 to 20 posts a day must land reach, not just exist.** Volume gives you more shots, and the algorithms reward consistency, but most posts do little. Hitting hundreds, then a thousand-plus trials a month means a real content machine with strong hooks, and a few clips that travel. Posting is necessary, distribution is the actual job. This is on me, and it is the biggest single unknown.
2. **Conversion has to hold around a third.** That needs a smooth trial, ideally a card captured at signup, and a first-week experience so useful they do not want to stop. Mostly a product and onboarding job.
3. **Churn has to stay low,** 6 to 8%. Trades are price sensitive and busy. Retention is product, not marketing.
4. **You have to be able to service the volume.** Hundreds, then thousands of free trials is a support and infra load. Vercel Pro, WhatsApp templates, and the AI bill all scale with it.

If those hold, Aggressive, a **£234k run-rate exiting December**, is a real outcome. If conversion or reach underdelivers, you slide toward Base or Conservative, which are still strong from a standing start.

## On casting wider than trades

You want to push across all angles, trades, coffee shops, anyone self-employed. The upside is a much bigger pool. Roughly four million plus self-employed people in the UK, not just the trades.

The caution: the product, the language, and the receipt categories are built sharp for **trades**, and a sharp wedge converts better than a broad mush. My steer: **lead with trades as the spearhead** because that is where the message bites hardest and the word of mouth is densest, and let it **spill outward** into the wider self-employed (the cafe owner, the mobile hairdresser, the personal trainer) as the content proves itself. Broaden the audience, but keep the trade voice as the tip. Go broad too early and the message gets generic and conversion drops, which, as above, is the number that matters most.

## The marketing plan that produces it

I run content and ads. You run the streets. This is the aggressive version.

### Organic, 10 to 20 posts a day, I run it
- **Volume and spread:** daily short-form video as the engine (Reels, TikTok, Shorts), plus statics and carousels, the same core ideas recut per platform, across Instagram, TikTok, Facebook, LinkedIn, YouTube. At 10 to 20 a day that is a genuine content factory, which is why we build the bank first.
- **Angles, all of them:** the receipt-shoebox pain, the 20-second demo, MTD explained simply, trust and anti-scam, the time-back stories, quick tips, myth-busting, behind the scenes of the build. Wide enough to catch trades first and the broader self-employed next.
- **Distribution, not just posting:** trade Facebook groups, local self-employed groups, Reddit, comment sections, and replying usefully everywhere sole traders ask about tax. Reach is earned, not assumed.

### Paid ads, I run them, phased from August
Start only once organic shows which hooks convert, so we pay to scale winners, not to learn. Spend grows from a small test toward four figures a month in the aggressive case, always behind a proven message.

### You, in person, the trust multiplier
Cards with a QR or wa.me link straight into the trial, handed out at merchants, Screwfix queues, sites, cafes, and every trade you know. In-person converts far better than cold ads because the trust is already there, and with a free trial the ask is tiny: "scan this, try it free for a month."

### Referrals
A free month is highly shareable. Make referring one tap, and it compounds as a slice of the base every month with no extra spend.

## What I would actually aim for

Treat **Aggressive as the target** now that the trial is in, **Base as the realistic floor**, and **Blitz as the prize** if the content genuinely catches. Plan your cash and your servicing capacity around Base so you are never caught short, and push like hell for Aggressive. Exiting December somewhere between a **£156k and £234k run-rate** from nothing in five and a half months would be a serious result, and a strong base to raise from or to keep compounding.

The honest one-liner: the free trial makes these aggressive numbers credible, and trial-to-paid conversion is the number that decides which column you land in. Everything we build and post should serve that one metric.
