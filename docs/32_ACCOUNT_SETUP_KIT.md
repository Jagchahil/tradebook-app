## My calls on how we win, since you asked me to run it

You told me to post however I think drives sales, conversion, and retention. Here is the plan I am running.

### Platform priority, weighted for conversion not vanity
UK trades skew to a 30 to 55 crowd who live on Facebook, are increasingly on TikTok, and check Instagram. So I am weighting effort like this:

1. **Facebook, heaviest.** The Page plus relentless value in trade groups. This is where the buyers are and where trust and word of mouth travel fastest for trades. Highest conversion per post.
2. **TikTok, for reach.** Cheapest organic reach for a new account, fills the top of the funnel and feeds everything else. High volume, punchy hooks.
3. **Instagram, secondary.** Reels reposted from TikTok, plus carousels and trust content. Good for the people who check you out before signing up.
4. **YouTube Shorts, evergreen.** The same clips, working for you in search for months. Low extra effort, long tail.
5. **LinkedIn, light.** A couple of posts a week on the going-legit and MTD angle, for the more organised sole trader.

### Conversion levers I am building into the content
- Every post ends on the **free trial**, framed as "no card games, try it free for a month."
- A **trust piece pinned** on each profile, because trades check before they trust.
- The **demo** front and centre, because seeing how stupidly easy it is converts better than telling.
- Lead with **trades** as the sharp wedge, spill to the wider self-employed once a hook proves itself.

### Retention, because a sale you lose in month two is not a sale
Conversion gets them in, retention keeps the MRR. Retention is mostly product, but content does real work here too, so I am running a quiet **retention track** alongside the growth posts:
- "Get the most out of Lekhio" tips, so new users hit the value fast and stick.
- Seasonal nudges, quarter-end and tax-deadline content that reminds them why they pay.
- Wins and stories from real users once we have them, which reassure the wavering.
- The biggest retention lever sits in the product, the first-week WhatsApp experience. I will flag content and product ideas that reduce churn as the numbers come in.

The whole machine points at one number, **trials started that convert and stay**. I will watch it daily and move effort to whatever is feeding it.
