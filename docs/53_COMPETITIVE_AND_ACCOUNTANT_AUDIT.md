# 53: Exhaustive Audit. Where Lekhio Stands vs Competitors and Real Accountants

> A grounded, honest assessment as of 29 June 2026. Built from a full read of our own code and docs, plus current market research. The aim is not to flatter Lekhio. It is to know exactly where we win, where we lose, and what must be fixed before we ask a tradesperson to pay.

---

## 1. The one-paragraph verdict

Lekhio has the best front door in the market for a UK tradesperson: capture by WhatsApp photo and voice, no app discipline required, everything categorised automatically, one flat price that replaces a stack of tools, and a free set of genuinely useful tools plus a start-to-finish business-setup concierge that no competitor offers. That is a real, defensible edge for our exact customer. But there is one gap that matters more than all the others right now: **we prepare the quarterly figures, we do not yet submit them to HMRC.** Making Tax Digital for Income Tax went live on 6 April 2026, and the first quarterly update for £50k+ sole traders is due by 7 August 2026. Several competitors can file today. Until our HMRC submission path is recognised and switched on, we are the best place to keep the books but not yet the place that closes the loop. Close that gap and the case for choosing Lekhio over anyone, software or accountant, becomes very hard to argue against.

---

## 2. The market moment, and why it cuts both ways

MTD for Income Tax is now law and live. From 6 April 2026, sole traders and landlords with qualifying turnover over £50,000 must keep digital records and send HMRC four quarterly updates through recognised software. The threshold drops to £30,000 in April 2027 and £20,000 in April 2028, so the addressable market roughly triples over two years. The first quarter (6 April to 5 July 2026) must be filed by 7 August 2026.

This is the wedge: millions of tradespeople who have never used software are being forced to, on a deadline, right now. It is also the risk: the thing they are being forced to do is **submit**, and that is the one thing we have not switched on yet. Every day between now and HMRC recognition is a day a £50k+ electrician picks a tool that can file. We must treat HMRC recognition as the single most urgent item on the roadmap.

## 4. Real accountants

What a sole trader pays a human today:

- One-off self assessment: **£150 to £350 plus VAT**.
- Ongoing monthly support: **£30 to £80** for bookkeeping plus self assessment, **£50 to £150** for fuller support, with budget online firms from about **£24.50/mo**.
- All-in annual: **£150 to £1,200** depending on complexity.

Where the accountant wins: a human relationship, judgement on grey areas, someone who is legally your agent and can be held to account, and they will actually file. For a complex or higher-earning trade, that reassurance is worth real money.

Where the accountant loses, and where we are structurally better: they are not there at 7pm on a Tuesday when the receipt is in your hand. They make you hoard a carrier bag of receipts and dump it on them in January. They charge per question, so people stop asking. They do not categorise as you go, do not give you a live tax figure, and do not help you set the money aside. MTD makes this worse for them and better for us: quarterly filing means four touchpoints a year instead of one, and the manual, shoebox-to-spreadsheet model does not scale to that cadence at £30/mo. We turn the quarterly burden into a thirty-second WhatsApp habit.

## 5. Where Lekhio is genuinely, structurally better

These are real and shipped in our code today, not slideware.

The capture experience is the best in the category for our customer. A photo or a voice note on the app every tradesperson already has, parsed by AI, categorised, stored, with a plain confirmation. No new app to open, no form, no discipline. For a plasterer with wet hands on site, that is the difference between doing the books and not.

It is genuinely all-in-one at one price. Bookkeeping, invoicing, mileage, diary and reminders, CIS tracking, a live tax figure and quarterly prep are in one £12.99 chat. The market makes you stack three to five tools and logins to get the same, for more money.

CIS is done properly. We log gross income and the deduction separately, so the deduction is tracked as tax already paid and surfaced as a refund tracker. Most generic apps treat a trade's CIS as an afterthought. For subcontractors this alone is a reason to switch.

We help before they even have a business. The free register-your-business concierge walks a new sole trader or company through registration, UTR, SIC codes, bank, email, insurance, certs and pension, with "claim it on Lekhio" nudges throughout. No competitor and no high-street accountant packages the *start* of the journey like this.

The free tools are real lead magnets. A no-login self-employed tax calculator and an invoice and quote generator that work instantly, plus plain-English "can I claim it?" guidance and a "file your own return" walkthrough. These pull in and build trust with people who are not ready to pay yet, at zero AI cost to us.

We are built for trades, not adapted for them. Trade-specific expense categories, per-trade SEO pages, the language and examples of electricians, plumbers and builders. Xero and Sage are general ledgers wearing a sole-trader hat.

The economics let us keep the promise. At a roughly 90% gross margin we can afford generous trials, free tools and human-quality help without nickel-and-diming, which is exactly where accountants and capped-receipt apps lose people.

## 6. Where we are lacking, honestly

Ranked by how much it threatens a sale.

1. **We cannot submit to HMRC yet. This is existential given MTD is live.** We prepare the quarterly figures and the user approves them, but there is no HMRC API integration in the code, no recognition, no submission. A £50k+ trade who needs to file by 7 August 2026 cannot complete the job with us today. Competitors from QuickBooks to free Pie.tax can. Top priority by a distance.
2. **The mobile app has no real login.** It signs in anonymously; phone one-time-code verification is not built. We cannot safely attach a paying subscription or sensitive financial data to an unverified anonymous account. Note: an earlier doc claimed OTP was in place. It is not. This must be fixed before real users.
3. **No live bank feed.** Open Banking via TrueLayer is only dormant database scaffolding. Competitors auto-import transactions; we rely on capture. Capture is our wedge, but the absence of a bank feed will be raised in comparisons.
4. **Mobile billing is a stub, and app-store rules complicate it.** Web subscription billing is now built and real. The in-app subscribe screen still just lets the user through, and Apple and Google may require their in-app purchase rails rather than Stripe inside the app. Needs a deliberate decision.
5. **WhatsApp is not confirmed in production.** The webhook and client are code-complete and hardened, but a live Meta Business number, app review and approved templates are a configuration and approval step we have not evidenced as done.
6. **The WhatsApp mechanic is not exclusive.** Accounted already does AI bookkeeping over WhatsApp. We should not market "we use WhatsApp" as the differentiator; we should market the trade depth, the all-in-one, the concierge and the price.
7. **Minor: the billing portal endpoint resolves a Stripe customer from an unauthenticated email.** Low severity, but worth gating behind real auth when #2 lands.

## 7. So why does a tradesperson choose us over anyone?

Over the cheap apps (QuickBooks, Pie, Coconut): because logging your books should not mean learning software. They give you another app and another login. We give you a WhatsApp chat you already know, that also sets up your business, tracks your CIS properly, and answers your money questions in plain English, for one price.

Over the big platforms (Xero, Sage, FreeAgent): because you are one person with a van, not a finance department. They are powerful and overbuilt for you, and priced for growth you do not need yet. We are the whole job, simpler and cheaper, made for your trade.

Over a high-street accountant: because we are there the moment the receipt is in your hand, not five months later. We categorise as you go, show your tax bill growing in real time so there is no January shock, help you set the money aside, and never charge you per question. For most sole traders under the complexity line, that is better and far cheaper than £30 to £150 a month.

The honest caveat that makes this credible: today that pitch is true for keeping the books and preparing the quarter. It becomes unanswerable the moment we can also file. That is the work.

## 8. Priority roadmap to make the case airtight

1. **HMRC MTD for Income Tax recognition and the submission path.** Everything else is secondary. Pursue recognition, build the quarterly update and final declaration submission with the required fraud-prevention headers, keep the human-approval gate in front of it. This converts "prepare" into "done."
2. **Real phone one-time-code login** so accounts and subscriptions attach to a verified person.
3. **Decide and build mobile billing** (web Checkout link vs Apple/Google in-app purchase) so the app can convert, not just the website.
4. **Confirm WhatsApp production** (Meta business, number, templates) and live keys so the core loop runs for real users.
5. **Open Banking via TrueLayer** to remove the last "but it does not see my bank" objection.
6. **Reframe marketing** around trade depth, all-in-one, concierge and price, not "WhatsApp", since that mechanic is now contested.
7. **Harden the billing portal** behind real auth and lock the founder discount to verified register-flow signups.

Do 1 to 4 and Lekhio is not just the nicest way to keep the books. It is the complete, cheaper, friendlier replacement for both the app stack and the accountant, for the exact moment the whole market is being forced to choose.
