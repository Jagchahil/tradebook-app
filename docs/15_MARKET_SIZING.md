# 15: Market Sizing

> How big the UK sole trader market is and how many customers Lekhio can realistically win. For when we get to sales and marketing. Figures gathered June 2026. Treat the headline counts as directional and re-check before any fundraising or board use. This is a working estimate, not a forecast.

---

## The universe (TAM)

- The UK had about 5.5 million private sector businesses at the start of 2024.
- Roughly 3.1 million of those are sole proprietorships (sole traders).
- About 4.2 million people work as solo self employed overall (the wider pool, including those who do not register as a formal business).
- Sole traders are the fastest growing slice, up about 139,000 (5%) in the last year.

At £12.99 a month (£348 a year), even a small share of this base is a large business.

---

## Who is realistically reachable (SAM)

Our wedge is Making Tax Digital, and the timing is the whole story. MTD for Income Tax becomes mandatory in phases, based on qualifying income (turnover, not profit):

| From | Qualifying income over | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| April 2026 | £50,000 | First wave mandated. HMRC has estimated this at very roughly 780,000 taxpayers. |
| April 2027 | £30,000 | Pool grows into the millions. |
| April 2028 | £20,000 | Most active sole traders are pulled in. |

By 2028 a large share of sole traders will be legally required to keep digital records and send a short quarterly update. Call the genuinely serviceable pool around 2 to 2.5 million sole traders who must have a tool, plus everyone under the threshold who simply wants painless bookkeeping and invoicing.

Once mandated, a person must stay in MTD for at least three years, so the requirement is sticky once it lands.

---

## What we can realistically win (SOM)

Micro business software adoption for a newcomer tends to land between 0.1% and about 1% of the addressable base in the first few years. We compete with FreeAgent, Coconut, QuickBooks and Xero self employed tiers, and Sage.

Against the roughly 3.1 million sole trader base:

| Share | Customers | ARR at £348 each |
|---|---|---|
| 0.1% | ~3,100 | ~£1.1m |
| 0.5% | ~15,500 | ~£5.4m |
| 1% | ~31,000 | ~£10.8m |
| 3% | ~93,000 | ~£32m |

A grounded trajectory for a well executed WhatsApp first product riding the MTD deadline:

- Year 1: hundreds to low thousands of paying users.
- Year 3: 10,000 to 30,000 paying if acquisition and retention hold.
- Upside: 50,000 or more if the WhatsApp angle becomes the reason people switch.

---

## The honest caveats

Three things decide whether we hit the top or the bottom of that range.

1. **Churn.** Micro businesses churn hard, and many go dormant. Retention is the number to watch, not signups.
2. **CAC versus value.** A customer is worth £348 a year. Acquisition has to be cheap, viral, or word of mouth. The WhatsApp referral loop is our best lever here.
3. **Competition waking up.** The MTD mandate creates demand, but it also pushes every incumbent to chase the same people. Our defensibility is genuinely doing the whole job through WhatsApp with zero data entry. That is what could let us punch above 1%.

---

## The takeaway

The market is big enough that we do not need a large share to build a real business. Capturing even 0.5% to 1% of UK sole traders is a £5m to £11m ARR company, and the MTD mandate is a once in a generation tailwind landing exactly as we launch.
