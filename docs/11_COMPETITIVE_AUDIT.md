# 11: Competitive Audit of UK Bookkeeping and Tax Software

> Researched June 2026 from real review evidence: Trustpilot, Capterra, App Store, Google Play, Reddit, ContractorUK, AccountingWeb, vendor pricing pages, and gov.uk. The goal is to find what people actually hate about the tools they use now, then make sure Lekhio does not repeat it. Sources are listed at the end. Where a quote could not be read word for word behind a login wall, it is paraphrased and the source is still named.

---

## The headline

The market is large, established, and quietly resented. The same complaints come up again and again across nearly every product: prices that keep rising, support you cannot get a human on, clunky software, and receipt scanning that does not work. Making Tax Digital is about to force hundreds of thousands of non digital sole traders into paid software they do not want. That is the opening. The winning move is not to be the cheapest filing tool, because that is already a price war. It is to remove the record keeping work entirely with the WhatsApp capture loop, hold a flat honest price, and give people a real human in the same chat. That hits the three sorest nerves in the market at once.

## What each one does, and where it hurts

**Xero.** The scalable, accountant friendly choice, now £16 to £65 a month after a 10 to 17 percent rise in September 2025. The loudest complaint is no inbound phone support, help is ticket only, which is useless when a payment fails at month end. Long term users report cost nearly doubling, with expenses and projects locked to dearer plans. Bank feeds drop and need reconnecting. Receipt capture needs the separate Hubdoc app. Strong product, not built for a busy person on site.

**QuickBooks.** The best mobile app in the category and a strong self employed pitch, which makes its problems instructive. The anger is about price: roughly 75 percent up over four years for some users, called "annual price gouging" on Intuit's own forum. Support gives "a different answer every time you call." There is a forum thread literally titled "Is in-app receipt scanning as awful for others as it is for me," reporting misread totals and failure on faded thermal receipts. Cancelling is hard and refunds are not pro rated.

**FreeAgent.** Free forever for NatWest, RBS and Mettle customers, which is a real distribution moat, otherwise £19 a month for sole traders. Praised for UK support from trained accountants. The recurring gripes are slow or disconnecting bank feeds with a 90 day re authentication, weak integrations that users have asked to fix for years, and fixed expense categories that feel rigid.

**Sage.** Big trusted brand with payroll bundled and, usefully for trades, CIS for subcontractors built in. The reviews are rough: cancellation is phone only with 40 minute holds and billing that continues after you leave, support is called rude and hard to reach, Sage 50 is widely called outdated and slow in 2025, and there is a verified AccountingWeb thread describing four months of transactions disappearing. Its receipt capture, AutoEntry, is metered by credits, not truly included.

**Coconut.** Simple sole trader and CIS tool, now built around MTD for Income Tax, £16.99 a month. Two things stand out. It killed its own banking product in 2022, which forced users to re plumb their accounts, and its price crept from about £3 to £16.99 over the years, which users resent. Almost no Reddit presence, which is itself a sign of low awareness.

**Crunch.** Software plus real accountants for a fixed fee, £27 a month and up. When it works people love it. The recurring problem is the promise not landing: a ContractorUK user reported 180 emails over three months and "not once was I contacted by an accountant," support quality described as degrading over six years, and a painful onboarding with historical data import.

**The rest.** ANNA pushes free MTD filing as a hook and has genuinely fast support, but suffers account freezes during onboarding. Tide is a free account with a bookkeeping add on and a bad reputation for suspending accounts without explanation. FreshBooks has the best invoicing but is US centric for UK tax. Zoho Books is cheap with a generous free tier but slow and prone to US date formats leaking into a UK product. Smaller MTD tools exist, from untied to cheap spreadsheet bridging at about £36 a year, which is the price floor for plain filing.

## Making Tax Digital, the context that creates the opening

Verified against gov.uk in mid 2026.

- It is now live. From 6 April 2026, sole traders with gross income over £50,000 must keep digital records and send quarterly updates. The threshold drops to £30,000 in April 2027 and £20,000 in April 2028.
- Qualifying income is gross turnover, not profit. A trader turning over £55,000 is caught even if they net far less.
- It means five submissions a year per business, four quarterly updates plus a final declaration. Tax is still paid on the usual dates, so the "tax four times a year" fear is a myth, but the paperwork really does multiply.
- Paper records no longer satisfy the law, and the free gov.uk filing route ends for those in scope.

What sole traders are angry about: the end of free filing, a petition to delay gathered over 20,000 signatures and was refused, the extra quarterly burden, and the simple fact that most of them are not digital. Around a third still use pen and paper and only about one in ten use cloud accounting. Only about 30 percent clearly understand what MTD even involves.

That is Lekhio's exact customer. The mandate is pushing a non digital, on site tradesperson into compulsory software and more frequent filing, and they hate the cost and the friction. We remove the friction.

## What this means we must do

Pulled out so it does not get lost.

1. **Hold the price.** One flat price, everything in. Our whole brand dies the first time someone posts a screenshot of a Lekhio price hike next to a QuickBooks one. If a rise is ever forced, grandfather existing users.
2. **Never meter receipts.** Unlimited capture, included. Sage charging per receipt is a gift to us. Do not copy it.
3. **Keep a human reachable in the chat.** This is the single most defensible advantage. Protect it as you scale. Crunch and QuickBooks show how support quietly rots and takes the brand with it.
4. **Guard receipt accuracy.** It is the feature rivals fail at. Use the confidence score to catch weak reads and re check them. Faded thermal receipts are the test that matters.
5. **Keep onboarding to seconds and cancellation to one tap.** Both are competitor pain points we already beat. Do not regress.
6. **Stay plain and British.** No jargon, no US formats, GBP, UK tax year. The brand voice rules already enforce this.
7. **Be honest about MTD.** Use an HMRC recognised submission path, never imply HMRC endorses us, always keep the human approval step before anything is filed, and tell people plainly that MTD means quarterly updates, not paying tax four times a year. This is already in the non negotiables. The market is full of confusion we can win trust by clearing up.
8. **Do not become a bank.** Holding money brings KYC freezes, the thing that sinks Tide and ANNA in reviews. Staying a pure back office tool sidesteps it.
