# 104. The Lekhio Doctrine. What we are, what we refuse to be, and what we say.

> Written 11 July 2026, out of a long session with Jag on brand and vision. This is the layer that sits above every other doc. Doc 07 says what Lekhio looks like. Doc 103 says how much goes on a screen. Docs 68, 91 and 04 say which channels we use. **This doc says what Lekhio IS, and it is the thing all of those should be checked against.** Writing rule holds throughout: no em dashes, no en dashes, no hyphens used as dashes.

---

## Why this doc exists

Jag ran the vision past another AI and got back the opening chapters of a founder's manifesto. Twenty six chapters. "The AI Operating System for Business." Mental load, the Apple influence, an origin story, a ten year roadmap, an org chart.

It was beautifully written and almost entirely empty. No numbers, no sources, no falsifiable claim, and it violated our own writing rule in every paragraph. It was also, in every meaningful way, a worse version of doc 98, which already said the same thing with the FCA's actual capital requirements, Tide's actual customer count, and a labelled confidence gradient.

But it surfaced three things that were genuinely missing from our docs, and one of them is the best line anyone has written about this company. So rather than throw the whole thing out, here is what survived, sharpened, and made true.

**The standing lesson: a hundred page vision document written two weeks before launch is not strategy. It is the most enjoyable possible way to avoid launching.** This doc is deliberately short.

---

## 1. The one line. Everything else is downstream of it.

> **Lekhio is not software you buy. It is the first employee a business ever hires.**

This is the single most important sentence in the company and it should be the first thing anyone hears.

It works because it changes what we are compared to. As software, we are a £12.99 app next to a £7 Xero and a £0 Monzo bundle, and we lose that fight on price forever. As an employee, we are £12.99 a month next to an accountant at £500 to £2,000 a year who answers on Tuesdays. Same product. Completely different question in the customer's head.

**Everything in the group vision follows from it.** The seven verticals in doc 98 are not "products we could bolt on." They are **the job description of the employee.** A good chief of staff does not hand you off to a different vendor when the task changes. That is the entire argument for one roof, and it is why "the business for businesses" is a real category rather than a slogan.

---

## 2. What we will never call ourselves

**Not "the AI Operating System for Business."**

Three reasons, and they are not stylistic.

1. **A plasterer has no idea what it means.** It is a phrase written for investors by people who have never met a customer.
2. **It is not differentiating.** Every AI startup on earth is claiming that phrase this year, including several with a billion dollars behind them.
3. **The metaphor is wrong, and wrong in an important way.** An operating system is passive. It sits there and you run apps on it. **You are still the operator.** That quietly preserves the exact thing we exist to destroy.

Today, every business tool assumes a human sits in the middle moving information between them. Xero does not talk to the bank, the founder does. The accountant does not chase HMRC on their own initiative, the founder chases the accountant. **The founder is the API.** That is the disease.

**Lekhio's bet is that the agent becomes the operator and the human becomes the approver.** That is not an operating system. That is an employee.

---

## 3. Apple. What we take, and where we go further.

Apple is the inspiration, not the template. And the honest reading of Jobs cuts against the manifesto instinct, not for it.

Jobs did not launch with a manifesto about personal computing for humanity. He launched with "1,000 songs in your pocket" and let the ecosystem story be told afterwards, once the iPod worked. When he returned in 1997 he killed roughly 70% of the product line before he said a single visionary word in public. **His method was subtraction.**

**What we take:**

- Hide the complexity. The customer should never meet an AI model, an API, or a data pipeline. They should meet a thing that works.
- People do not know what they need until you show them. We decide for them.
- Sell the outcome, never the technology. Nobody buys a tax engine. They buy a quiet head.
- Own the ecosystem, but only with things that are individually the best. Apple was an ecosystem of things that each won their category. The iPod was not one of seven things Apple was working on. **This is the check on doc 98's seven verticals: breadth only earns its place if each piece is genuinely good. "We do everything a bit" is how good companies die.**

**Where we go further than Apple, and this is the part that matters:**

Apple could remove buttons because the cost of being wrong was mild. Get the click wheel wrong and someone skips a track. **We operate on a person's tax, their bank account, their company, and eventually the sale of the thing they spent their life building.** Get it wrong there and they get a penalty, or worse.

"We know what they need better than they do" is exactly right about the interface and exactly dangerous about the outcome.

Which gives us the resolution, and it is stricter and cleaner than Apple's, because Apple never had to hand anything back:

> **Take away every button. Except one.**
>
> **Lekhio decides everything that is reversible. The user decides everything that is not.**

## 4. The line. Put it on the wall.

Jag's own words, and the best thing said in the whole session:

> ### One less button at a time. Until only one is left. Approve.

**Why it is more than a slogan.** It makes progress measurable in the opposite direction to every other software company. They ship features. We ship removals. A roadmap where the product gets *smaller* every quarter and does more is genuinely hard for a competitor to copy, because copying it means giving something up.

Doc 103 is the tactical application of this line to a screen. This doc is where the line itself lives.

## 5. The two kinds of authorisation

Jag's steer on the bank: "everything needs to be done with authorisation. We explain what we want to do, and you hit run."

He was using the word to mean one of two things. **Lekhio needs both, and confusing them is how this company could get itself in real trouble.**

**The user's authorisation.** Our moat. The rule in CLAUDE.md. Nothing irreversible happens without a human thumb. It is the thing nobody else has the discipline to build, and it is why a customer would ever give an agent the keys to a bank account.

**The FCA's authorisation.** Our gate. It decides **what we are permitted to prepare in the first place.**

**Pressing run needs both.**

### The correction that has to be written down, because it is the dangerous one

**The approval gate is NOT a regulatory shield. On investments, it puts us further inside regulation, not outside it.**

Under article 53 of the Regulated Activities Order, *advising on investments* is the act of making the recommendation. It is not the execution. If Rakha says "I am going to move £500 into this fund, hit run," **the recommendation has already been made.** A human thumb on the button does not undo it. And when they press run, we have then also performed article 25, *arranging deals in investments*, which is a separate regulated activity.

So the agentic prepare-then-approve model on investments needs **two** permissions where a plain advisor needs one.

This is not exotic. It is exactly what Nutmeg, Moneyfarm and Wealthify are: a machine that prepares a recommendation and executes it on consent. They all hold article 53 and article 25. The category is real and achievable. It is simply not free, and it is not a loophole.

Full regulatory map, activity by activity, is in doc 98.

## 6. The category

> **Lekhio is the business for businesses.**

One agentic front end for everything a self employed person or small company needs to start, run, fund, and eventually exit a business, instead of five logins with five companies. Doc 98 has the seven pieces, the sequencing, the licensing, and the numbers.

The check on it, from section 3 above: **each piece must be individually excellent, or it does not ship.** Breadth without depth is not an ecosystem, it is a bundle, and bundles lose to specialists.

## 7. The front door. The thing that is genuinely bigger than we had written down.

This is buried in doc 98 and it should not be. It is the strategic idea of the whole company.

**Every competitor in this category, and every accountant in Britain, only starts counting a customer once they already have a business.** Tide, Xero, QuickBooks, Monzo, Accounted. Their funnel begins at "you are already trading."

- The UK has roughly **5 to 6 million** people who already have a business. That is the market everyone is fighting over.
- Roughly **33% of UK adults, on the order of 17 to 18 million people, intend to start a business or side hustle within the next twelve months.**
- **Nobody is talking to them.**

The product is a free, agentic Lekhio on WhatsApp that anyone can talk to before they have registered anything. Sole trader or limited? What will I actually owe? What should I charge? What insurance do I need?

**Why this is a moat and not just a nice idea:** every hour we spend earning trust with someone before they have a business is an hour our competitors structurally cannot spend. When that person does start, we are not a cold signup. We are the obvious next step. **It is the cheapest customer acquisition channel in the category and it is ours alone.**

The one number to prove: guide-to-incorporation conversion. Unproven until measured. Test it small before assuming a rate.

**The one line version, which is the whole company in a sentence:**

> **You do not need a business to talk to Lekhio.**

## 8. The moat, restated so it cannot drift

Decided 7 July (doc 87), and nothing in this session changed it:

**The money is in Rakha and the depth of the knowledge base. It is not in the capture channel.**

Receipt OCR over WhatsApp is table stakes now. Accounted does it. Receiptor does it. Dext does it. What nobody else has is a proactive agent backed by a store of UK tax rules, reliefs and case law that gets deeper every week.

**The rule: everything we build makes Rakha smarter or the knowledge deeper. Nothing else earns a place on the roadmap.**

Note the tension with section 6, and hold it consciously: **depth and breadth pull against each other.** The resolution is sequencing. The niche version, being the best thing in the world at self employed money, earns the right to the group. Not the other way round.

## 9. The marketing doctrine

Doc 68 and 91 own the channels and the cadence. Doc 04 owns the funnel. **This section owns what we actually say.** These six angles supersede any positioning line in doc 04 that contradicts them.

### The six angles

**1. The employee, not the app.** The biggest reframe we have, and nobody in the category is using it. It kills the price objection before it is made.

> Your first hire costs £12.99 a month.
> An accountant costs you hundreds a year and answers on Tuesdays. Lekhio costs less than a Nando's and answers now.
> Every business needs a bookkeeper. Yours never sleeps, never invoices you, and never loses a receipt.

**2. One less button.** The brand campaign, not the performance campaign. The About page, the manifesto film, the thing that makes people feel something.

> One less button at a time.
> Most software adds. We take away.
> The best thing we shipped this year was something we deleted.

**3. The mental load.** The emotional one. It is not the paperwork, it is carrying it around all week. The eleven o'clock on the sofa ad.

> You're not bad at admin. You're just already working.
> The job doesn't finish when you get in the van.
> A hundred small things in your head. None of them are the reason you started.

**4. The honesty weapon.** Push this hardest. It is aggressive, it is true, and our rivals literally cannot say it back. Accounted files live and we do not. **Turn that into the attack instead of the weakness.**

> Any app that says it will do your tax for you is lying to you. HMRC holds you responsible. It always has.
> We prepare it. You press approve. That is the deal, and it never changes.
> Lekhio will never spend your money without asking. Not once. Not ever.

That last line is a promise a bank cannot make, an accountant cannot make, and an AI startup will not make. It is ours.

**5. Zero new habits.** Keep it stupid.

> If you can text your mum, you can do your books.
> No app to learn. You already know how to send a photo.
> Screwfix. 9:14am. £83.40. Logged. Get back to work.
> Text it. It's in your Lekhio.

**6. The front door.** Not for launch. This is the campaign that eventually makes Lekhio a household name, because it speaks to 17 million people instead of 1.3 million.

> You don't need a business to talk to Lekhio.
> Thinking about going out on your own? Ask us anything. It's free, and we'll tell you the truth.
> Most people never start because nobody ever explained it to them.

**Launch is built on angle 1 and angle 4.** The first changes what we are compared to. The second turns the thing everyone calls our weakness into the reason people trust us.

### What we never say

- **"The AI operating system for business."** See section 2.
- **"We file your tax for you."** It is a lie, and it is the one lie that could actually end us.
- **"Mental load."** That is the insight, not the copy. **Say the feeling. Never say the name of the feeling.**
- **Anything implying HMRC endorses us.** We are not endorsed and never will be.

> **Honesty is not a constraint we tolerate. It is the product we are selling.**

## 11. The standing questions

Ask these of anything anyone proposes, in this order.

1. **Does it make Rakha smarter or the knowledge deeper?** If not, why is it on the roadmap.
2. **What did we take out to make room for it?** (Doc 103.)
3. **Is it reversible?** If yes, do it and tell him plainly. If no, he approves. No exceptions.
4. **Would a plasterer up a ladder, with one hand on the rail, understand it in three seconds?**
5. **Is it true?** Not "is it defensible." True. If we would not be comfortable with HMRC, the FCA, and the customer all reading it, it does not ship.
