# 69: Retention Playbook

> Written 1 July 2026. Retention is the single number that decides whether Lekhio is a nice business or a generational one. This is the plan to make it a moat, grounded in what we have already built, with a year on year improvement path. Retention is not one feature, it is the compounding result of getting a handful of things right and never stopping.

---

## 1. Why this is the whole game

Churn caps the ceiling more than acquisition does. If you lose 5 in 100 customers a month, the most customers you can ever hold from a steady inflow is roughly twenty times your monthly adds, no matter how hard you pour in at the top. Halve the churn and you double the size of the company on the same marketing. Every serious software fortune is a retention story wearing an acquisition costume. So we obsess over keeping people, not just winning them.

Two numbers to separate. Logo churn is customers leaving. Revenue churn is pounds leaving, which can be lower if leavers are small and stayers upgrade, or even negative if stayers expand faster than others leave. The generational outcome needs revenue churn to trend toward zero and ideally go negative as the corporate tier and add ons arrive.

---

## 2. Why trades retention is winnable, the honest version

Micro business subscriptions usually churn hard, because the tools are optional and the pain they solve is dull. Lekhio is different in three ways that we should lean on deliberately.

**The pain is legally mandatory now.** MTD means quarterly filing is not optional. A tool tied to a legal obligation with recurring deadlines has a built in reason to be reopened four times a year plus year end. We did not create that hook, the government did. We just have to be the easiest way to meet it.

**The value accrues and cannot leave with them easily.** Every receipt, every mile, every CIS deduction a user logs becomes their tax record for the year. By month six they are not choosing whether to keep an app, they are choosing whether to abandon their own books mid year and start again somewhere else. That is a real switching cost, and it grows every week they use us.

**We hold money that is owed to them.** The CIS refund tracker and the reliefs finder are not features, they are reasons to stay. A subbie who can see the refund building toward them will not cancel the thing that is tracking their money.

The risk is the opposite side of the same coin. If a user does not reach that accrued value, if they sign up, log two receipts, and drift, none of the above protects us. So the entire retention job splits into two: get them to value fast, then make leaving cost them something.

---

## 3. What we have already built that drives retention

We are not starting from zero. The pieces already in place, and how each one holds a user:

- **The WhatsApp loop.** The lowest friction habit in the category. No app to open, no login, just a text. Habits form where friction is lowest, and a habit is retention.
- **The quarterly and annual tax cycle, with reminders and the cron nudges.** A built in reason to re engage on a schedule, and we already send the nudges.
- **CIS handling and the refund angle.** Money owed to the user, tracked, visible. Loss aversion working for us.
- **Invoicing and paid invoice tracking.** Once a trader runs their invoices through us, we are in their cash flow, not just their tax. That is an operational dependency, the stickiest kind.
- **The dashboard set aside and tax figures.** Certainty about a scary thing. People do not cancel the thing that tells them they are safe.
- **Gamification, wrapped, achievements, goals.** Light, but they add reasons to look and a sense of progress that is theirs.

The spine is strong. The work is to shorten time to value at the front and deepen lock in over the years.

---

## 4. The two jobs, and the levers under each

### Job one, get to value fast, this kills early churn

Most cancellations happen in the first thirty days, before the accrued value exists. Levers:

- **First value in under a minute.** The goal of onboarding is one logged, categorised entry as fast as possible. The first receipt or the first "spent 40 on diesel" is the aha. Measure time to first entry and drive it down.
- **The money moment, early.** Show a new user something they gained in week one, a claimable expense they would have missed, a refund starting to build, tax set aside so they are not caught out. Value they can feel beats features they have to learn.
- **Proactive, not passive.** The WhatsApp nudges should notice silence. If a user logged nothing for a week, a friendly prompt. If a deadline nears, a reminder with the number already prepared. Re engagement designed in, not left to chance.
- **Onboarding that assumes they are on site.** No forms. Voice and photo first. The product rule of the company applied to keeping people, not just winning them.

### Job two, make leaving cost something, this kills late churn

- **Their history is their asset.** Make the accrued year visible, so leaving visibly means losing it. A year summary they would have to rebuild elsewhere.
- **Bank feeds, when they come.** Once a trader connects their bank, retention steps up hard, because disconnecting and reconnecting elsewhere is real effort and their transactions flow automatically. This is the highest impact retention feature we do not yet have, see the roadmap.
- **Filing itself, once recognised.** The day we file the quarter for them behind their approval, leaving means re doing their tax somewhere else. That is maximal lock in, and it is the natural end state of the HMRC work already underway.
- **Annual billing.** An annual plan is a year of retention bought up front. Every user we move from monthly to annual is a year we do not have to re win. Push annual at the moment of highest trust, right after the first refund found or the first quarter filed.

### The quiet killer, involuntary churn

A large share of subscription churn is not a decision, it is a failed card. Cards expire, payments bounce. Without a recovery flow those users leave silently. A dunning sequence, retry the card, tell the user warmly, offer to update details, typically recovers a meaningful slice of churn for near zero effort. This is one of the highest return retention investments there is and it is pure plumbing. Build it before scaling paid ads, because paying to acquire users who then churn on a bounced card is money set on fire.

---

## 5. Year on year improvement roadmap

Constant improvement, each year adding a deeper reason to stay.

**Year one, 2026 to 2027, nail the loop and stop the leaks.** Time to first value under a minute. The week one money moment. Silence detecting nudges. A proper dunning and card recovery flow. Push annual billing at peak trust. Target, get monthly churn trending down from the launch assumption toward the mid single digits, and prove it with cohort curves.

**Year two, 2027 to 2028, deepen the dependency.** Bank feeds and open banking, the biggest single retention lever available to us. Automated CIS refund claiming, not just tracking. A light accountant handoff so users who grow do not leave for a firm, they bring the firm onto us. Multi year history that makes us their permanent record.

**Year three onward, own the outcome.** Filing behind approval, the maximal lock in. The corporate and multi location tier that lifts revenue per customer and drives revenue churn negative. Team and subcontractor features that create network pull, where one tradesperson brings the people they pay.

The pattern is deliberate. Each year we move from holding a habit, to holding their data, to holding their bank, to holding their filing. Each step is harder to walk away from than the last.

---

## 6. What to measure, and the honest scoreboard

- **Cohort retention curves.** For each month's joiners, what share are still paying at month 1, 3, 6, 12. This is the truth. Everything else is commentary.
- **Time to first value.** Minutes from signup to first logged entry. The leading indicator of month one retention.
- **Logo churn and revenue churn, monthly.** Watch both. Aim revenue churn toward zero, then negative.
- **Net revenue retention.** Revenue from a cohort a year on versus at signup, including upgrades and losses. Above 100 percent is the signal of a generational business.
- **Involuntary churn share.** How much of churn is failed payments. If it is high, dunning is the cheapest growth you will ever buy.
- **Quarterly active around deadlines.** Are users re engaging on the tax cycle we know is coming.
- **Annual plan share.** Rising annual mix is retention banked.

Report these to the founder monthly, plain and unflattering. A retention problem hidden is a company lost slowly.

---

## 7. The one line to remember

We do not retain users by adding features, we retain them by getting them to value in the first minute and by being woven a little deeper into their money every year, until leaving means abandoning their own books, their bank feed, and their filing. Build that, and the churn assumption in the model stops being a risk and starts being the moat.
