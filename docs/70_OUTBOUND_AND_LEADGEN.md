# 70: Outbound and Lead Generation, Compliant and Aggressive

> Written 1 July 2026. The founder wants to reach the many trades who are discoverable online, by cold email and DM, and asked about mining public contact data. The instinct, reach them where they already are, is right. The specific method, cold blasting scraped contacts, is not legal for our market and would be uniquely damaging for a tax compliance brand. This doc gives the honest legal position and then the aggressive version that stays inside the line. This is guidance, not legal advice, take formal advice before running any outbound programme.

---

## 1. The hard legal fact that decides everything

Under the UK Privacy and Electronic Communications Regulations, marketing by electronic mail, which includes email, SMS, and social media direct messages, needs the recipient's consent when they are an "individual subscriber". The Information Commissioner's Office is explicit that **sole traders and ordinary partnerships are treated as individuals**, not as businesses. Our entire core market, self employed tradespeople and sole traders, are individual subscribers.

So the plain reading: we cannot lawfully send unsolicited marketing emails, texts, or DMs to sole traders without their prior consent, or the narrow soft opt in that only applies to our own existing customers. Only "corporate subscribers", limited companies and limited liability partnerships, can be cold emailed for business to business marketing without consent, and even then we must identify ourselves and offer an opt out.

Two more walls behind that one. Scraping Instagram or websites for contacts is processing personal data under UK GDPR, and "publicly visible" does not mean "free to use for marketing", we would still need a lawful basis, a privacy notice, and to honour people's rights. It also breaches the platforms' terms of service, and Instagram bans accounts that mass message or automate DMs very quickly. And there is a brand risk that dwarfs the legal one: we are selling tax compliance and trust to people who are nervous about getting things wrong. Being reported to the ICO for spam, "the tax compliance app that broke privacy law", would be a self inflicted wound we might not recover from. It is precisely the headline a competitor would pray for.

Note also that the Data (Use and Access) Act received Royal Assent in June 2026 and touches this area, so the detail can move. Another reason to take live legal advice before any programme, not to rely on this note.

---

## 2. The reframe, from cold blasting to consent at scale

The winning move is not to find a clever way to spam people who did not ask. It is to make so many of them raise their hand that we never need to. We turn reach into consent, then market to the consented all day long, entirely within the rules. That is both legal and, done well, more effective, because a hand raiser converts far better than a cold contact ever would.

---

## 3. The aggressive, compliant playbook

**1. Turn the free tools into a consent engine, this is the unlock.** We already have a tax calculator, a CIS calculator, an invoice generator, and can I claim guides on the site. Add a value exchange to each: "See the CIS refund you could be owed, enter your email to get your result and your MTD deadline reminders", with a clear, unbundled marketing opt in. Every trade who uses a tool becomes a consented contact we can email lawfully and forever. Run the organic content engine hard to drive traffic into these tools. This converts reach into a marketable list at scale, and it is the single highest leverage legal outbound asset we can build.

**2. Content led inbound and genuine engagement, not automated DMs.** Show up in the places trades gather, comment on their posts, answer the tax questions they ask in public, be useful. DM people who follow us or engage with us first, that is warm, wanted, and outside the cold marketing rules. Never automate it, never buy follower lists, never mass message. One real reply from a nervous plumber who just got an MTD letter is worth a thousand blasts.

**3. Real world outbound is wide open, use it hard.** PECR restricts electronic marketing, not the physical world. Door to door, leaflets and posters at builders' merchants and trade counters, cards under windscreen wipers on work vans, a stand at trade shows, sponsoring a local trades group. This is exactly the founder's door to door plan, and it is unrestricted, cheap, and high trust. Lean into it as the primary cold channel while the consent list builds.

**4. Corporate subscriber outbound, the one cold channel we can run electronically.** Limited company and LLP trades and contractors can be cold emailed for B2B marketing without consent, with identification and an opt out. Companies House is a legitimate public data source with a lawful basis for this. It is a smaller slice of the trades market than sole traders, but it is a real, legal cold email channel, useful for the later corporate and multi location product especially.

**5. Partnerships borrow other people's consent.** Wholesalers, tool suppliers, van leasing firms, trade bodies, and accountants already have consented audiences of exactly our people. A referral or bundle deal reaches them through a party they already trust, with the consent already handled. Higher trust, zero legal exposure.

**6. Referral loops turn customers into a channel.** Give a month, get a month, or similar, so every happy tradesperson brings the mates they work alongside. Word of mouth is the most trusted and most compliant acquisition there is.

---

## 4. What we will and will not build

We will build the consent engine, the lead magnets and opt in capture on the free tools, the email programme to the consented list, the referral loop, and the corporate subscriber B2B email programme on a proper lawful basis. We will not build a scraper, we will not run automated cold DMs, and we will not cold email or text sole traders without consent. Not because we are timid, but because the compliant path is both safer and better, and because the one thing we can never afford to lose is the trust that we are the people who keep you on the right side of the taxman.
