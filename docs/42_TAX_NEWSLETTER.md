# 42: The Monthly Tax Newsletter

> A monthly "what changed in tax, in plain English" digest, by email and a short WhatsApp alert. It positions Lekhio as the trusted voice for trades, not just a tool, and it keeps you in front of your users every month. The first issue is written below, ready to send. From now on it drafts itself automatically.

---

## How it runs

- **It drafts itself monthly.** A scheduled task, "lekhio-monthly-tax-newsletter", runs on the 1st of each month. It researches the latest UK tax and HMRC changes for the self-employed, writes the email and a short WhatsApp alert in Lekhio voice, and saves them to `content/newsletter_YYYY-MM.md`. You review and send. (It runs while the desktop app is open, like the other scheduled tasks.)
- **You stay in control of sending.** Nothing goes out automatically. You read the draft, tweak if you like, and send.

## How to send it

- **Email:** for a real newsletter you want a tool with list management and a one click unsubscribe, so use **Mailchimp, Klaviyo, or MailerLite**, or send via **Resend** broadcasts. Do not blast it from a personal inbox.
- **WhatsApp:** a monthly broadcast is a Marketing message, so it needs an approved **marketing template** and the user must have opted in to marketing. Keep it short, a headline plus a link, not the whole newsletter. Template below.

## Opt in and compliance

- **Add a newsletter opt in** at signup (a simple checkbox on `/start`, "Send me monthly tax tips"). Until then, only email users who clearly expect it, and always include an unsubscribe link. This keeps you clean under UK GDPR and PECR.
- **WhatsApp marketing** needs explicit opt in and has a per message cost. Gate it on a marketing preference, the same way nudges are gated.
- **Tone and accuracy:** general information, never personal tax advice, never imply HMRC endorsement. The drafting task is instructed to follow this.

### Email

**Subject:** The tax changes that actually matter to you this year

Quick one. A few real changes landed this tax year that affect you as a sole trader. Here is what changed, what it means, and what to do. Two minutes, no jargon.

**1. Mileage jumped to 55p a mile**
From April 2026 the business mileage rate rose to 55p for your first 10,000 miles, then 25p after that. That is the first rise in 15 years. Motorbikes are 24p, bikes 20p.
What it means: if you drive for work, every mile is now worth more off your tax bill.
What to do: log every business mile. With Lekhio you just text "drove 24 miles" and it is claimed at the right rate.

**2. Making Tax Digital is now live for higher earners**
From April 2026, if your turnover is over £50,000 you must keep digital records and send four short quarterly updates instead of one big return. The £30,000 crowd joins in April 2027.
The good news: HMRC is not handing out late penalty points for the first 12 months, a grace period while everyone settles in.
What to do: keep digital records now. Lekhio does that for you as you go, so you are already ready.

**3. Working from home, do not get caught out by the headlines**
You may have seen that the working from home tax relief was scrapped from April 2026. Read the small print: that is the relief for employees. If you are self employed, your home office claim is different and still stands. You can still claim the simplified flat rate, £10 to £26 a month depending on hours, or a share of your actual costs.
What to do: keep claiming it. Text "worked 90 hours from home" and Lekhio logs it.

**4. National Insurance, the quiet bit of good news**
Class 2 National Insurance is now voluntary for most self employed people, and Class 4 sits at 6% on profits over £12,570, down from a couple of years ago.
What it means: a little less coming off your profit than before.

That is the lot for this month. Lekhio keeps your records ready for all of it, so when the deadline comes there is nothing to scramble for. Reply if you have a question, a real person answers.

Text it. It is in your Lekhio.
